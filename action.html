Organizations to Support

These worldwide organizations are doing serious work on clean energy, climate, and the environment. Find some that aligns with what you care about most.

Global

350.org

A global movement working to accelerate the clean energy transition and end the fossil fuel era through grassroots campaigns, activist training, and international organizing.

Advocacy Global
Greenpeace

One of the world's most recognizable environmental organizations, known for direct action campaigns on climate, deforestation, ocean protection, and the phase-out of fossil fuels across dozens of countries.

Advocacy Global
Climate Action Network

A worldwide network of over 1,800 civil society organizations working to promote government and individual action to limit climate change to ecologically sustainable levels.

Advocacy Global
Climate Reality Project

Founded by Al Gore, this organization trains and mobilizes grassroots climate leaders around the world to drive local action on clean energy and environmental justice.

Education Global
Environmental Defense Fund

A science-driven nonprofit tackling climate change and pollution globally. Works across governments, companies, and communities to reduce emissions and strengthen environmental policy.

Policy Global
Clean Air Task Force

Focused on reducing the most impactful greenhouse gas emissions through technical analysis and policy advocacy. Their methane reduction work has influenced international climate negotiations.

Policy Global
Rocky Mountain Institute

An independent nonprofit accelerating the clean energy transition through research, analysis, and collaboration with businesses, governments, and communities worldwide.

Policy Global
World Resources Institute

A global research organization working on sustainable food, land, water, energy, cities, and climate. Produces widely cited data and analysis on emissions, deforestation, and energy transitions.

Research Global
Carbon180

A nonprofit focused on carbon removal solutions through research, policy, and advocacy. Their work helped shape the US direct air capture tax credit and continues to influence carbon removal markets and regulation worldwide.

Technology Global
Earthjustice

The leading nonprofit environmental law organization. Uses litigation and policy advocacy to enforce environmental protections and defend communities on the frontlines of pollution and climate impacts.

Legal Global
The Nature Conservancy

One of the largest conservation organizations in the world, protecting land and water across 79 countries through science, partnerships, and the scaling of nature-based climate solutions.

Conservation Global
World Wildlife Fund

Protects wildlife, restores ecosystems, and advances nature-based climate solutions across nearly 100 countries. One of the world's largest and most recognized conservation organizations.

Conservation Global
Rainforest Alliance

Works to conserve biodiversity and improve livelihoods by promoting sustainable land use, responsible supply chains, and the protection of tropical forests as critical climate assets.

Conservation Global
RARE Conservation

Focuses on changing human behavior to protect nature and climate, using social science and community-led approaches to drive conservation outcomes in the places that need it most.

Conservation Global
Indigenous Climate Action

An Indigenous-led organization guided by knowledge keepers, water protectors, and land defenders. Believes that Indigenous rights and knowledge systems are critical to solving the climate crisis and achieving climate justice.

Community Global

United States

Sierra Club

One of the oldest and largest environmental organizations in the US. Works to solve the climate crisis through a clean energy economy and protect public lands, waters, and communities.

Advocacy US
Sunrise Movement

A youth-led political movement fighting to stop climate change and create millions of jobs in the process. Known for grassroots organizing, direct action, and electoral engagement.

Advocacy US
American Clean Power

Unites the US clean energy industry to address climate change and drive economic growth, working at the federal, state, and local levels to accelerate the deployment of renewable energy.

Advocacy US

Legal US

The American Council for an Energy-Efficient Economy. Leverages energy efficiency to advance economic prosperity, energy security, and environmental protection for all Americans through policy and research.

Policy US
Rewiring America

A nonprofit dedicated to electrifying American homes, businesses, and communities. Develops accessible data and tools to help individuals and policymakers accelerate the transition away from fossil fuels.

Technology US
EESI

The Environmental and Energy Study Institute. Provides non-partisan education and information on energy and environmental policy to members of Congress, their staff, and the public.

Education US
Ocean Conservancy

Works to protect the ocean from climate change, plastic pollution, and industrial threats. Advocates for science-based policies and leads large-scale ocean and coastal cleanup efforts.

Conservation US
The Solutions Project

Funds and amplifies frontline climate justice solutions, directing flexible grants to organizations led by Black, Indigenous, and people of color communities, those most affected by climate change.

Community US
Climate Justice Alliance

A coalition of frontline communities and organizations building a just transition away from extractive economic systems, centering racial and economic justice at the heart of climate action.

Community US

UNEP

The United Nations Environment Programme. Coordinates global environmental action, drives climate agreements, and monitors the state of the planet across 193 member countries.

Policy International
C40 Cities

A network of nearly 100 of the world's largest cities committed to delivering climate action in line with science. Helps cities share solutions, access finance, and accelerate the urban clean energy transition.

Community International

The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change. The world's leading scientific body on climate change, producing comprehensive assessments of climate science that form the basis for international policy. A reference body, not a donor organization.

Research International
IEA

Research International
IRENA

The International Renewable Energy Agency. Supports countries in the global energy transition by providing data, analysis, and technical assistance on renewable energy deployment worldwide. A reference body, not a donor organization.

Individual Actions

You don't have to do everything. Pick one thing that fits your life and start there. Actions compound, and your impact will be greater than you'll ever know.

Learn and Stay Informed

Follow credible climate sources

Bookmark Carbon Brief, Canary Media, and Yale E360 for regular, accurate updates on clean energy and climate. Understanding what is happening is the first step to doing something about it.

Track what your government is doing

Check The Watch regularly to follow federal actions on energy and climate. Knowing what policies are being made or reversed keeps you informed and ready to respond.

Speak Up and Advocate

Contact your representatives

Elected officials respond to constituent pressure. A direct call or email about specific climate legislation is one of the most effective individual actions you can take.

Find your representative →

Vote on climate

Climate policy is decided at every level of government, federal, state, and local. Research candidates' positions on clean energy, climate, and environmental justice. Make it a priority at the ballot.

Register or check your status →

Talk about it

Talk about the climate crisis we face with the people around you, friends, family, and your community. Conversation and discourse shifts culture and policy more than you know.

Reduce Your Impact

Switch to clean energy at home

Many utility providers now offer clean energy plans, or you can switch to a clean energy supplier. It is one of the most high-impact changes a household can make.

Reduce consumption and waste

The production of goods accounts for a significant share of global emissions. Buying less, buying used, and reducing food waste are practical and immediate.

Rethink transportation

Transportation is one of the largest sources of personal emissions. Walking, cycling, public transit, or reducing flights where possible all make a measurable difference.

Support and Give

Donate to effective organizations

Financial support to well-run global organizations multiplies your impact far beyond what individual actions alone can achieve. Small, regular donations add up.

Browse organizations above →

Volunteer locally

Most climate and environmental organizations need volunteers for campaigns, events, and outreach. Local involvement builds power and keeps you connected to the cause.

Divest from fossil fuels

If you have savings or investments, look into whether your bank or fund holds fossil fuel assets. Moving money toward clean energy investments sends a message to the market.
